Research Areas

Dissertation

Cognitive Biases in LLM-Assisted Audit Judgments

Florida International University · DBA Program

Large language models are rapidly entering audit workflows — from drafting risk assessments to evaluating control narratives. But LLMs don't eliminate human bias; they may amplify, mask, or redirect it. My dissertation investigates how anchoring bias propagates when auditors use LLM-generated outputs as starting points for professional judgment.

  • How does anchoring bias shift when auditors receive LLM-generated risk ratings vs. peer benchmarks?
  • Do LLM outputs create a false sense of precision that reduces healthy skepticism?
  • What debiasing interventions (structured prompts, adversarial review) are most effective?
  • How should audit methodology adapt to human-AI collaborative judgment?
Research Interest

AI Governance & Model Risk in Financial Services

Enterprise Risk · SR 11-7 Compliance

Financial institutions face a governance gap: existing model risk frameworks (SR 11-7, SS1/23) were designed for statistical models, not foundation models. I study how audit and risk functions must evolve to provide meaningful oversight of AI systems that are probabilistic, opaque, and rapidly changing.

  • Model Risk Management frameworks for LLMs and generative AI
  • Board-level AI governance documentation and reporting structures
  • Explainability requirements for AI-assisted credit, fraud, and compliance decisions
  • Vendor due diligence and third-party AI risk assessment
Applied Research

RAG-Powered Audit Quality Assurance

LangChain · Python · Enterprise AI

Built a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) tool for audit quality assurance at Citigroup that earned the "Delivers with Pride" award. This work explores the practical challenges of deploying AI in a highly regulated environment where accuracy, traceability, and auditability are non-negotiable.

  • Designing retrieval pipelines for regulatory documents and audit workpapers
  • Ensuring citation fidelity — every AI output must trace to source evidence
  • Building human-in-the-loop workflows that enhance (not replace) auditor judgment
  • Measuring and mitigating hallucination risk in compliance-critical contexts
